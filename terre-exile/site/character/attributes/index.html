    Attributes

    Attributes are meant to change a bit throughout the character's journey by progressing in their pathes.
    Average (and base) value is 25 for humanoïds.
    All attributes have a passive improvement equal to the character's level.

    Primary Attributes

    Image Nom de l'attribut Realm Element Description
    Strength (STR) Body Fire Measures physical power for tasks like lifting and breaking.
    Dexterity (DEX) Body Water Indicates precision and skill in hand movements.
    Agility (AGI) Body Air Reflects speed and flexibility for graceful movement.

    Strength (STR)

    This a primary attribute.
    One of the attribute's of the body.
    Attached to fire element.
    		Strength represents a character’s raw physical power, though it doesn’t directly indicate muscle mass. A character can be strong without an imposing stature; Stature better reflects body mass. Strength is crucial for wielding impact weapons, such as hammers and axes, where a powerful swing makes all the difference. It also supports demanding physical tasks like lifting heavy objects, breaking down barriers, and climbing steep surfaces. Beyond brute force, Strength enhances physical skills and interactions like blacksmithing and intimidation, where a solid physique becomes a key asset.

    Dexterity (DEX)

    This a primary attribute.
    One of the attribute's of the body.
    Attached to water element.
    		Dexterity measures a character’s hand-eye coordination and precision, essential for wielding guarded weapons like swords, where control and finesse are key. This attribute is vital for fine manipulations, enabling skills like lockpicking, recycling, juggling, and sabotage. Dexterity defines a character's ability to perform delicate tasks with accuracy, making it invaluable in situations that require precision and a steady hand.

    Agility (AGI)

    This a primary attribute.
    One of the attribute's of the body.
    Attached to air element.
    		Agility reflects a character's speed, flexibility, and body control, allowing them to maneuver with grace and fluidity. This attribute is key when wielding polearms, such as spears or halberds, where nimbleness and precision footwork are essential for effective handling. Agility enables actions requiring flexibility and leg dexterity, making it invaluable for dodging obstacles, maintaining balance, and performing movements that demand a swift, controlled response from the body.

    Constitution (CON)

    This a primary attribute.
    One of the attribute's of the body.
    Attached to earth element.
    		Constitution represents a character's health, vitality, and resilience, crucial for enduring both physical strain and pain. It plays a key role in using defensive equipment like shields, where sustained endurance is essential. Constitution measures a character's stamina and toughness, enabling them to resist illness, recover from injuries, and withstand prolonged exertion.

    Perception (PER)

    This a primary attribute.
    One of the attribute's of the body.
    Attached to void element.
    		Perception measures a character’s sensory awareness and attentiveness to subtle details in their environment. It’s also the primary attribute for ranged attacks, as keen perception is essential for accuracy over distance. Additionally, Perception enhances a character's responsiveness, making them more attuned to the rhythms of combat and better able to react quickly to shifts and openings.

    Charisma (CHA)

    This a primary attribute.
    One of the attribute's of the spirit.
    Attached to fire element.
    		Charisma describes the strength of a character’s personality and their ability to impact others. It’s the key attribute for persuasion, allowing a character to positively or negatively influence, motivate, or demoralize those around them. Charisma empowers a character to inspire confidence or instill doubt, making it essential for social interactions where influence is paramount.

    Intelligence (INT)

    This a primary attribute.
    One of the attribute's of the spirit.
    Attached to water element.
    		Intelligence indicates a character's ability to reason, learn, and solve complex problems. It is essential for reading between the lines of a document, writing efficiently by perfectly rendering information and inserting hidden messages, as well as connecting elements during an investigation. Intelligence also plays a crucial role in the ability to remember things, making it easier to remember and recall important information.

    Cunning (CUN)

    This a primary attribute.
    One of the attribute's of the spirit.
    Attached to air element.
    		Cunning represents a character's ingenuity and responsiveness, their ability to think quickly and adapt to new situations. Cunning is particularly useful for navigating complex urban environments and interacting with domestic animals, allowing ease in manipulating social elements and human-built environments.

    Willpower (WIL)

    This a primary attribute.
    One of the attribute's of the spirit.
    Attached to earth element.
    		Willpower measures a character's determination and mental strength, allowing them to resist manipulation and mental fatigue. It is also crucial for tapping into one's inner determination to accomplish remarkable feats that defy expectations.

    Wisdom (WIS)

    This a primary attribute.
    One of the attribute's of the spirit.
    Attached to void element.
    		Wisdom reflects a character's experience and judgment, their ability to make thoughtful decisions based on their experiences. It plays an essential role in healing, navigation in rural environments, and interaction with wild animals, allowing harmony with the natural environment.

    Secundary Attributes

    Image Nom de l'attribut Description
    		Magic (MAG) Represents magical prowess and arcane ability.
    		Logic (LOG) Reflects analytical and scientific thinking.
    		Luck (LCK) Represents chance and fortune; impacts unpredictable events.
    		Wealth (WEL) Indicates the character's financial resources and assets.
    		Balance (BAL) Reveals inner harmony; impacts recovery and resilience.

    Magic (MAG)

    This a secundary attribute.
    Also one of the attribute's of the spirit.
    Attached to arcane element.
    		Magic represents a character's affinity with arcane forces, enabling them to cast spells and interact with enchanted objects and magical trinkets. This attribute is essential for understanding and harnessing mystical energies, whether to perform rituals, manipulate magical items, or perceive hidden magical properties. Magic defines a character’s capacity to wield and shape the supernatural.

    Logic (LOG)

    This a secundary attribute.
    Also one of the attribute's of the spirit.
    Attached to metal element.
    		Logic represents a character’s mastery of advanced science, often associated with steam-powered technologies, yet encompassing simpler scientific principles as well. It grants the technocrat the ability to perceive and decode the flows of steam energy, making Logic essential for understanding and manipulating mechanical and technological systems. More than simple reasoning, Logic embodies cognitive adaptability, equipping characters with the mental flexibility to innovate and respond to complex challenges.

    Luck (LCK)

    This a secundary attribute.
    Not aligned with body or spirit.
    Attached to no element.
    		Most of the time this attribute is not used to check success, but there can be few exception to that (gambling for instance). Luck represents chance and fortune that influence a character's life, affecting events in unpredictable ways. Its base value is calculated from the other attributes, the higher the total of the other attributes, the lower the chance.

    Wealth (WEL)

    This a secundary attribute.
    Not aligned with body or spirit.
    Attached to no element.
    		Most of the time this attribute is not used to check success, but there can be few exception to that (bribing for instance). Fortune represents the wealth of the character, a mixture of regular income, heritage, economy... See how economics works for more details about Wealth specific mecanics.

    Balance (BAL)

    This a secundary attribute.
    Aligned with both body and spirit.
    Attached to no element.
    		Most of the time this attribute is not used to check success, but there can be few exception to that (resisting corruption for instance). Balance reveals inner harmony, guiding recovery and resilience. The well-balanced bounce back with vigor, while the unbalanced struggle, their flame flickering under the test. Its base value is calculated from the other attributes: It's the average between the third highest and the third lowest attributes.
